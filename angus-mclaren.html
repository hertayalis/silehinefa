Angus McLaren- Biography

Angus McLaren is an American actor best known to the globe for his roles as Nathan Rafter in the Australian comedy-drama series “Packed to the Rafters” (2008-2013), and most recently as Lance Salisbury in the TV series “Home and Away” (2018-2019), among other ventures.

Angus McLaren- Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Siblings, Education

Angus McLaren, of Scottish descent, was born on November 3, 1988, in Wonthaggi, Victoria, Australia.

He grew up on a dairy farm near Loch with his two brothers, Rhett and Aidan, and became interested in the performing arts at a young age, beginning his career at the age of 12. He attended Mary Mackillop College Leongatha and was a member of the Leongatha Lyric Theatre. Later, he would pursue a Bachelor of Acting degree at the Western Australian Academy of Performing Arts in Perth, Western Australia.

Angus McLaren- Professional Career

Angus’ career began by coincidence in 2000 when he was cast as Jason Cassidy in the television series “Something in the Air.”

McLaren auditioned for the job despite having no prior experience, and he starred in seven episodes of the award-winning drama series from 2000 to 2001. McLaren took another step forward in 2002 when he was cast as Jamie Clarke in the long-running soap opera “Neighbours,” and two years later he was cast as Degenhardt Bell in 26 episodes of the TV sci-fi series “Silversun.”

Ascension to prominence

Angus was cast in the role that provided him the push he needed in 2006, when he began playing Lewis in the TV fantasy drama series “H20: Just Add Water” (2006-2010), eventually starring in 66 episodes of the award-winning series.

Concurrently, his next major job occurred in 2008, when he was cast in the role of Nathan Rafter, which recognized him as an actor and established him as an Australian celebrity. From 2008 to 2013, he starred in 102 episodes of the “Packed to the Rafters” series, which increased his notoriety and riches.

Following the conclusion of the series, Angus continued his career by attending acting lessons in New York, Melbourne, Sydney, and Los Angeles, as well as the Western Australian Academy of Performing Arts.

In 2016, he played William Graham in the short film “Quartermaine,” and in 2017, he played Dr. Toke in the TV comedy-drama series “Doctor Doctor.”

Consistent Success

In 2017, he also played Pat in the TV comedy series “600 Bottles of Wine,” and then continued with film roles, first as Carpet Burn in the comedy-drama film “The Merger,” starring Damian Callinan, Kate Mulvany, and John Howard, and then as Eddie in the historical thriller-drama film “Hotel Mumbai,” starring Dev Patel, Armie Hammer, and Nazanin Boniadi.

In addition, Angus participated in 18 episodes of another long-running soap opera, “Home and Away,” in 2018. He co-starred in the romantic comedy film “The Naked Wanderer” with John Cleese and Natasha Liu Bordizzo in 2019, and he is now working on the spin-off series “Back to the Rafters,” in which he will reprise the character of Nathan Rafter; the series is set to release late in 2020.
