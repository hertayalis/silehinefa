The Mellon Family Legacy

The Mellon family’s fortune originated with Thomas Mellon, who founded T. Mellon & Sons’ Bank in Pittsburgh in the 19th century. His son, Andrew W. Mellon, expanded the family’s wealth through banking and eventually served as the U.S. Secretary of the Treasury. The family’s interests have since branched into various sectors, including finance, real estate, and art.

Historical Context of Wealth

The Mellons are often compared to the Rockefellers and Carnegies in terms of their historical impact on American industry and culture. Their wealth has been passed down through generations, with each heir contributing to its growth and diversification.
