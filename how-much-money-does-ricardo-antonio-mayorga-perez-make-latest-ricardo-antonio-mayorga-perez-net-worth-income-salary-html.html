Granada, Nicaragua native Ricardo Antonio Mayorga Perez, known professionally as “El Matador,” was born on October 3, 1973, and is a professional boxer from Nicaragua. He is a former World Boxing Association/World Boxing Council Welterweight champion as well as a former World Boxing Council light middleweight champion. He has a professional record of 29-8, with 23 knockouts, 1 tie, and 1 no-contest. He was featured on the cover of Ring Magazine for the first time in the December 2003 edition, which was published in October of that year. “The wildest guy in the sport: Mayorga lights up boxing,” stated the front cover of the magazine. Outside of the ring, he is well-known for his unrelenting trash-talking before most of his bouts, as well as for his smoking and drinking habits. He began his professional career on August 29, 1993, when he was beaten by Humberto Aranda in the sixth round in a TKO. Since making his professional debut in 1993, he has won three championships, all of which have come in the Middleweight and Welterweight categories.
